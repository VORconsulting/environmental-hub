GHG Action

How You Can Support GHG Reduction

GHG emissions are influenced by energy use, travel choices, resource use and everyday operational behaviour. Small actions help support broader emissions reduction.

At Work

Reduce unnecessary energy use

Energy use is one of the main drivers of operational emissions.

Energy Electricity Efficiency
  • Switch off lights, monitors and non-essential equipment when not needed.
  • Use meeting rooms and shared spaces efficiently.
  • Report areas that are consistently overcooled, overheated or inefficient.

Support responsible travel choices

Travel choices can influence emissions linked to business operations.

Travel Meetings Choices
  • Use virtual meetings where they are effective and appropriate.
  • Combine trips or meetings where practical to avoid unnecessary travel.
  • Consider lower-emission travel options where available and suitable.

Reduce avoidable resource use

Lower resource consumption supports overall environmental performance.

Paper Waste Resources
  • Reduce unnecessary paper use and printing.
  • Sort waste correctly to support recycling and diversion.
  • Use office resources responsibly and avoid unnecessary consumption.
Beyond the Workplace

Lower energy use at home

Everyday energy choices support broader emissions reduction habits.

  • Switch off unused appliances and reduce standby power where possible.
  • Use efficient lighting and energy-saving settings on devices.
  • Choose energy-efficient appliances when replacing equipment.

Make lower-carbon choices

Personal choices can help normalise lower-emission behaviour over time.

  • Reduce unnecessary travel where practical.
  • Repair, reuse and recycle items before replacing them.
  • Explore renewable or lower-carbon options where suitable and affordable.
Why this matters: Emissions reduction is strongest when organisational systems and everyday choices both support lower energy use, lower waste and more efficient operations.
