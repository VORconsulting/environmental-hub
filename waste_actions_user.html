Waste Action

How You Can Improve Waste Performance

Simple actions at work and beyond the workplace can help reduce waste, improve recycling, and support better diversion from landfill.

At Work

Sort waste correctly

Correct separation helps ensure materials can actually be recycled or composted.

RecyclingSeparationBins
  • Use the correct bin for recyclables, general waste and food waste where available.
  • Keep liquids and food residue out of dry recycling streams.
  • Follow local signage at waste points.

Reduce unnecessary paper use

Reducing paper at source prevents waste before it is created.

PrintingPaperDigital
  • Print only when needed.
  • Use double-sided printing where printing is necessary.
  • Use digital review, sharing and approval processes where practical.

Avoid contaminating recyclables

Small mistakes can cause recyclable materials to be sent to landfill.

Clean materialsFood wasteQuality
  • Empty bottles, cups and containers before recycling.
  • Keep food-contaminated items out of dry recycling bins.
  • Ask or check signage if unsure where an item should go.
Beyond the Workplace

Reduce household waste

Everyday choices can reduce the amount of waste generated at home.

  • Choose reusable items where practical.
  • Plan purchases to reduce avoidable food waste.
  • Repair, donate or repurpose items before discarding them.

Recycle and compost where possible

Keeping useful materials out of landfill supports better resource recovery.

  • Separate recyclables according to local collection rules.
  • Compost food and garden waste where facilities are available.
  • Dispose of e-waste, batteries and hazardous items through appropriate channels.
Why this matters: Reducing waste and sorting it correctly helps increase diversion from landfill, reduces contamination, and supports better environmental performance.
