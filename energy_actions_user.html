How You Can Improve Energy Performance

A mobile-friendly concept page that can be linked to a QR code within the Environmental Performance & Action Hub.

At Work

Switch off what is not needed

Reduce unnecessary energy use during and after working hours.

Lighting Monitors Meeting rooms
  • Turn off lights in unused rooms where automatic controls are not in place.
  • Shut down monitors and non-essential equipment at the end of the day.
  • Check shared spaces before leaving.

Use cooling and heating responsibly

Comfort settings can have a significant impact on building energy demand.

HVAC Temperature Doors
  • Keep doors and windows closed when air conditioning is operating.
  • Avoid unnecessary use of personal heaters or cooling devices.
  • Report areas that are consistently overcooled or overheated.

Use shared spaces efficiently

Room bookings and shared equipment affect how efficiently space is used.

Meeting rooms Occupancy Equipment
  • Release rooms that are no longer needed.
  • Use appropriately sized spaces for smaller meetings.
  • Turn off screens, projectors, and chargers when not in use.
Beyond the Workplace

Reduce energy use at home

Everyday household choices can lower overall energy demand.

  • Switch off unused appliances and avoid standby power where possible.
  • Use efficient lighting and energy-saving settings on devices.
  • Run major appliances only when needed and at efficient settings.

Make efficient choices over time

Longer-term decisions can strengthen resilience and reduce energy costs.

  • Choose energy-efficient appliances when replacing equipment.
  • Improve insulation, shading, or natural ventilation where feasible.
  • Explore solar or other suitable low-carbon options where practical.
Why this matters: Lower energy demand supports reduced emissions, improves efficiency, and strengthens the impact of renewable energy initiatives.
