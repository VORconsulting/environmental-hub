Water Action

How You Can Improve Water Performance

Simple everyday actions can help reduce avoidable water use and support Absa's operational water reduction pathway.

At Work

Report leaks quickly

Small leaks can become large water losses if they are not addressed.

Leaks Reporting Maintenance
  • Report dripping taps, running toilets or visible leaks as soon as possible.
  • Notify facilities teams about repeated or unusual water issues.
  • Do not assume someone else has already reported the issue.

Use water responsibly

Everyday water use affects total operational consumption.

Awareness Usage Facilities
  • Close taps fully after use.
  • Avoid unnecessary running water in kitchens and bathrooms.
  • Use water-efficient facilities as intended where they are available.

Support efficient shared spaces

Shared spaces work best when water issues are visible and reported.

Kitchens Bathrooms Shared spaces
  • Report blocked or faulty taps, toilets or water points.
  • Avoid leaving taps running in shared kitchens or bathrooms.
  • Encourage responsible use of water in shared office spaces.
Beyond the Workplace

Reduce water use at home

Household habits can reinforce responsible water behaviour.

  • Fix leaks and dripping taps promptly.
  • Use water-efficient fixtures and appliances where practical.
  • Run washing machines and dishwashers with full loads where possible.

Build water-smart habits

Small consistent actions help reduce avoidable water demand over time.

  • Use only the water needed for daily tasks.
  • Choose drought-resilient or lower-water garden options where relevant.
  • Stay aware of local water restrictions or conservation guidance.
Why this matters: Responsible water use helps reduce operational consumption, supports efficiency targets, and protects water as a shared resource.
