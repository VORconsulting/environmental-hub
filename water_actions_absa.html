Water Action

How Absa Is Improving Water Performance

Absa is improving water performance through better monitoring, efficiency measures, maintenance, and site-level action to reduce operational water consumption.

What Absa Is Doing

Leak Detection & Maintenance

Reducing avoidable water losses through better detection and faster response.

Leaks Maintenance Response
  • Identifying leaks and abnormal consumption patterns.
  • Supporting faster maintenance and repair processes.
  • Reducing avoidable water losses across operational facilities.

Efficient Fixtures & Controls

Improving water efficiency through building-level upgrades and controls.

Fixtures Efficiency Buildings
  • Using efficient taps, toilets and fittings where practical.
  • Improving controls to reduce unnecessary water use.
  • Prioritising high-consumption areas for efficiency opportunities.

Water Monitoring

Improving visibility of water consumption and progress against the target pathway.

Data Monitoring Trends
  • Tracking operational water consumption against the 2024 baseline.
  • Monitoring progress against the 2030 consumption target.
  • Using data to identify high-consumption facilities and trends.

Water Intensity Management

Linking water use to occupancy and operational activity.

Intensity FTE Performance
  • Tracking water intensity in litres per FTE.
  • Using intensity metrics to support fairer performance comparisons.
  • Aligning consumption reductions with operational occupancy patterns.
Looking Ahead

Targeted Water Efficiency

Water performance improves when monitoring, maintenance and behaviour change are managed together.

  • Strengthening site-level water performance visibility.
  • Prioritising interventions where savings potential is highest.
  • Maintaining alignment with the 2024–2030 water target pathway.
Why this matters: Reducing water consumption supports resource efficiency, lowers operational risk, and helps Absa progress toward its updated 2030 water targets.
