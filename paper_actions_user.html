Paper Action

How You Can Improve Paper Performance

Simple everyday choices can help maintain reduced paper use and support a more digital, efficient way of working.

At Work

Print only when needed

The most effective paper saving is avoiding unnecessary printing in the first place.

Printing Avoided use Documents
  • Ask whether a document really needs to be printed.
  • Use digital versions for review, reference and sharing where practical.
  • Avoid printing duplicate copies for meetings or circulation.

Use efficient print settings

When printing is necessary, small settings can reduce paper use.

Double-sided Drafts Efficiency
  • Use double-sided printing where possible.
  • Print only the pages you need.
  • Preview documents before printing to avoid mistakes and reprints.

Use digital workflows

Digital habits help keep paper reductions in place over time.

Digital Sharing Approvals
  • Share documents electronically instead of printing them.
  • Use digital notes, signatures and approval tools where available.
  • Store and retrieve records digitally when policy allows.
Beyond the Workplace

Reduce paper use at home

Household choices can also reduce paper demand and waste.

  • Choose electronic statements and receipts where practical.
  • Avoid unnecessary printing of travel, school or household documents.
  • Reuse scrap paper where appropriate before recycling it.

Recycle paper responsibly

Paper that is still used should be recovered where recycling systems are available.

  • Keep paper separate from wet or contaminated waste.
  • Recycle paper through the correct local channels.
  • Dispose of confidential documents using approved secure processes.
Why this matters: Reducing paper use helps avoid waste, supports digital efficiency, and protects the strong reduction already achieved against the 2018 baseline.
