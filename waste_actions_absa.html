How Absa Is Improving Waste Performance

Absa is improving waste performance by strengthening waste separation, increasing diversion from landfill, and improving visibility of waste streams across sites.

What Absa Is Doing

Waste Separation Systems

Improving how waste is separated at source to increase recycling and reduce contamination.

SeparationRecyclingContamination
  • Supporting clearer separation of recyclable and general waste streams.
  • Improving placement and visibility of waste collection points.
  • Reducing contamination that can prevent materials from being recycled.

Recycling & Composting Pathways

Strengthening the systems that divert waste away from landfill.

RecyclingCompostingLandfill diversion
  • Working with waste service providers to support diversion from landfill.
  • Identifying waste streams that can be recycled or composted.
  • Improving consistency of waste management practices across sites.

Paper Reduction

Reducing avoidable paper use as part of broader waste performance improvement.

PaperDigitalAvoided waste
  • Encouraging digital alternatives where practical.
  • Reducing unnecessary printing and duplication.
  • Supporting paper reduction as part of waste minimisation.

Waste Data Tracking

Improving visibility of waste performance to support better decisions.

DataTrackingPerformance
  • Tracking waste diverted from landfill over time.
  • Using data to identify sites or streams needing attention.
  • Supporting performance reporting against waste objectives.
Looking Ahead

Continuous Improvement

Waste performance improves when systems, service providers, data and behaviour work together.

  • Improving consistency of waste practices across locations.
  • Strengthening alignment between waste data and action planning.
  • Building awareness around correct waste separation.
Why this matters: Diverting waste from landfill reduces environmental impact, supports resource recovery, and helps make waste performance visible and actionable.
